Exports and Imports of Energy

Distribution of Energy has an obvious impact on energy trade. If a country is rich in energy and at the same time, consumes a lot. Then the country has very less energy to trade. Again, a country consuming less energy, can be a net exporter even though they profuce less energy.

Energy Consumption vs GDP

The linkage between energy and economic growth is a widely discussed topic. A country with higher GDP has better access to everything resulting in more consumption of energy.

Here, in the bubble plot showing data from 1990-2010, we can see all the countries have increased their GDP. Similarly, the energy consumption for those countries have also increased linearly. So we can clearly state that there is a clear linkage between economic prosperity and higher energy consumption.


What drives energy consumption?




Increased number of energy consumption has a direct impact on global climate change. Energy produced through burning of fossil fuel gives rise to atmospheric carbon di oxide. These increasing number of CO2 works as the key to climate change. In this later part of the dashboard, we will visualise more about CO2 and its impact on the Climate change.
